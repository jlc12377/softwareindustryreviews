Best HR & Payroll Software — 2026 Rankings

By Editorial Team Updated March 31, 2026 10 products ranked 56,000+ reviews analyzed
Methodology-based ranking · How we rank
We evaluated 10 HR and payroll platforms using a weighted methodology: User Satisfaction (30%), Payroll Accuracy & Compliance (25%), Employee Experience (20%), Integration Ecosystem (15%), and Pricing Transparency (10%). The HR tech market is undergoing rapid consolidation, with platform vendors racing to combine HRIS, payroll, benefits administration, and talent management into unified systems. Featured placements are clearly marked and do not influence organic rankings. Read our full methodology →
#1
Rippling
San Francisco, CA · SMB to Enterprise · Founded 2016
Best Unified PlatformHR + IT + FinanceCompound Automation
Rippling has emerged as the most ambitious platform in HR tech by unifying HR, IT device management, and finance (spend management, corporate cards, bill pay) into a single employee system of record. The "compound" approach means onboarding a new hire automatically provisions their laptop, sets up their apps, enrolls them in benefits, and adds them to payroll — in minutes, not days. The automation engine is the deepest in the category.

#2
Gusto
San Francisco, CA · SMB · Founded 2011
Best for Small BusinessFull-Service PayrollBenefits + Compliance
The gold standard for small business payroll and HR. Gusto handles full-service payroll (including tax filing in all 50 states), benefits administration, workers' comp, and basic HR tools with an interface that non-HR professionals can navigate. The Simple plan starts at $40/month + $6/person. Ideal for companies with 5–100 employees that want payroll and benefits handled without dedicated HR staff.

#3
BambooHR
Lindon, UT · SMB to Mid-Market · Founded 2008
Best Employee ExperiencePerformance ManagementATS Built-In
The most employee-friendly HRIS on the market. BambooHR combines core HR, payroll, time tracking, benefits, performance management, and applicant tracking in a clean interface that both HR teams and employees actually enjoy using. The employee self-service portal and mobile app consistently receive the highest satisfaction scores in the category. Best for organizations with 50–500 employees.

#4
ADP Workforce Now
Roseland, NJ · Mid-Market to Enterprise · Founded 1949
Most Trusted for Compliance75+ Years in PayrollGlobal Capabilities
The most established payroll provider in the world, processing payroll for one in six U.S. workers. ADP Workforce Now covers payroll, HR, benefits, talent, time, and compliance for mid-market organizations. Where ADP excels is compliance depth — tax filing accuracy, regulatory updates, and audit support built on 75+ years of institutional knowledge. The tradeoff: the interface feels dated compared to modern platforms like Rippling and Gusto.
