CRM software for small businesses is fundamentally different from enterprise CRM. Where enterprise CRM (Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics, Oracle CX) is built for complex multi-business-unit deployments with dedicated administrators, SMB CRM needs to be usable on day one by a single owner-operator or a 5-person sales team. That changes everything: pricing per user, onboarding time, default workflows, and which integrations matter.

We evaluated 38 CRM platforms targeting the SMB segment, then narrowed to the top 10 based on user satisfaction scores from G2, Capterra, and TrustRadius, pricing transparency, free tier strength, ease of use for non-technical owners, and feature depth in the core SMB CRM use cases — lead capture, deal tracking, contact management, email integration, and basic automation. The ranking below reflects 2026 market reality, with HubSpot's continued free-tier dominance and the rise of work management platforms (monday, ClickUp) competing for SMB CRM budget.

HubSpot CRM

The clear leader in SMB CRM — free forever, scales when you need it

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely free — unlimited users, unlimited contacts, no time limit. It includes contact management, deal pipelines, email tracking, meeting scheduling, and basic reporting. When you outgrow it, Sales Hub Starter ($20/user/mo) adds automation, sequences, and forecasting. The killer feature: tight integration with HubSpot's free marketing, service, and CMS hubs means SMBs can start free and scale into a complete business platform.

Zoho CRM

The most feature-rich CRM at the lowest price point

Zoho CRM gives SMBs enterprise-grade features (workflow automation, custom modules, AI-powered insights via Zia) at $14/user/mo — a fraction of Salesforce or HubSpot Professional. Best fit for SMBs already using other Zoho products (Books, Desk, Inventory) and willing to invest 1-2 weeks in setup.

FAQ

Common questions

What's the best free CRM for a brand new small business?
For most SMBs starting out, HubSpot CRM is the clear winner — unlimited users, unlimited contacts, no time limit, and a polished interface. The free tier alone is enough for many businesses for years. Zoho CRM (free for 3 users) and Freshsales (free for 3 users) are solid alternatives if you don't want the HubSpot ecosystem.
HubSpot vs Salesforce for small business — which is better?
For under 50 employees, HubSpot wins decisively. Salesforce is genuinely designed for enterprise — its starting "Starter" plan is $25/user/mo, but the platform's strength is in multi-team customization that SMBs don't need. HubSpot's free tier and $20/user Starter plan give SMBs 80% of the practical value at a fraction of the cost. Save Salesforce for when you exceed 100 employees and need real customization.
How much should a small business spend on CRM?
For SMBs under 25 employees, expect $0-50/user/month. Many businesses thrive on free tiers (HubSpot, Zoho, Freshsales) until they need automation or advanced reporting. Once you cross 10 employees and need workflow automation, plan for $20-50/user/month. The biggest cost mistake is over-buying — most SMBs use less than 30% of the features in their CRM.
Do these CRMs integrate with QuickBooks, Shopify, and Gmail?
All 10 CRMs on this list integrate with Gmail (most natively). For QuickBooks, HubSpot, Zoho, Pipedrive, and Freshsales have native integrations. For Shopify, HubSpot, Zoho, and EngageBay have native connections. For other integrations, most platforms support Zapier as a bridge to 5,000+ apps.
When should I upgrade from a spreadsheet to a real CRM?
If you're managing more than 50 active deals or contacts, missing follow-ups, or have more than one person who needs to see customer data, it's time. The cost of a free CRM is zero. The cost of a missed deal because it slipped through a spreadsheet crack is much higher. Start with HubSpot's free tier and migrate when you outgrow it.
